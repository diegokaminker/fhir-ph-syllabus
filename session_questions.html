FHIR Public Health Training 2025

Session Questions & Answers

Session 1: Context

Q1: What is the primary goal of healthcare interoperability?
A: To enable different healthcare systems to exchange and use information seamlessly, improving patient care coordination and reducing errors.
Q2: What are the three main challenges facing public health data exchange today?
A: 1) Fragmented data systems, 2) Lack of standardized data formats, 3) Limited real-time data sharing capabilities.
Q3: How does FHIR address the limitations of previous HL7 standards?
A: FHIR uses modern web technologies (REST APIs, JSON), is easier to implement, supports mobile apps, and provides better developer tools.
Q4: What is the significance of the 21st Century Cures Act for healthcare interoperability?
A: It mandates information blocking prevention, requires standardized APIs, and promotes patient access to their health data.
Q5: What role does public health play in the broader healthcare ecosystem?
A: Public health monitors population health, prevents disease outbreaks, and coordinates responses to health emergencies.
Q6: What are the key benefits of implementing FHIR in public health systems?
A: Improved data quality, faster outbreak detection, better resource allocation, and enhanced collaboration between agencies.
Q7: How does data modernization support public health objectives?
A: It enables real-time surveillance, predictive analytics, and evidence-based decision making for population health management.
Q8: What is the difference between clinical and public health data needs?
A: Clinical data focuses on individual patient care, while public health data focuses on population-level trends and interventions.
Q9: Why is standardization important for public health data exchange?
A: Standardization ensures data consistency, enables automated processing, and facilitates meaningful comparisons across jurisdictions.
Q10: What are the main barriers to implementing FHIR in public health organizations?
A: Legacy system constraints, limited technical expertise, funding constraints, and organizational resistance to change.

Session 2: Landscape

Q1: What are the main components of the US healthcare data landscape?
A: Electronic Health Records (EHRs), Health Information Exchanges (HIEs), public health systems, and various data standards organizations.
Q2: How has the evolution of HL7 standards impacted healthcare data exchange?
A: Each version (V2, V3, FHIR) has improved interoperability, with FHIR being the most modern and web-friendly approach.
Q3: What is the role of ONC in promoting healthcare interoperability?
A: ONC coordinates national efforts to implement health IT, develops standards, and promotes interoperability through policy and programs.
Q4: What are the key differences between HL7 V2 and FHIR?
A: V2 uses pipe-delimited text and is message-based, while FHIR uses JSON/XML and is resource-based with REST APIs.
Q5: How do Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) support public health?
A: HIEs aggregate data from multiple sources, enabling population health monitoring and public health surveillance.
Q6: What is the significance of the TEFCA framework?
A: TEFCA establishes a common agreement for nationwide health information exchange, enabling secure data sharing across networks.
Q7: How do public health agencies currently receive data from healthcare providers?
A: Through various methods including manual reporting, batch file transfers, and some real-time electronic reporting systems.
Q8: What are the main challenges in the current public health data landscape?
A: Fragmented systems, inconsistent data formats, delayed reporting, and limited real-time capabilities.
Q9: How does the CDC's Data Modernization Initiative address these challenges?
A: It focuses on modernizing public health data infrastructure, improving data quality, and enabling real-time data exchange.
Q10: What role do standards development organizations play in healthcare interoperability?
A: They develop and maintain technical standards, ensure consistency across implementations, and promote best practices.

Session 3: Foundational FHIR Part 1

Q1: What is a FHIR Resource and what are its key characteristics?
A: A FHIR Resource is a discrete unit of healthcare information with a defined structure, metadata, and standardized representation.
Q2: What are the four levels of FHIR conformance?
A: 1) Abstract, 2) Template, 3) Implementation, 4) Conformance Resource.
Q3: What is the difference between a FHIR Profile and a FHIR Resource?
A: A Resource is the base definition, while a Profile constrains and extends a Resource for specific use cases.
Q4: What are the main data types used in FHIR?
A: Primitive types (string, integer, boolean), Complex types (Address, HumanName), and Resource types.
Q5: What is the purpose of FHIR Extensions?
A: Extensions allow adding custom data to FHIR Resources when standard elements don't meet specific requirements.
Q6: How does FHIR handle terminology and coding?
A: FHIR uses CodeableConcept and Coding data types to represent standardized terminologies like SNOMED CT, LOINC, etc.
Q7: What is the significance of FHIR's RESTful API approach?
A: It uses standard HTTP methods (GET, POST, PUT, DELETE) making it familiar to web developers and easier to implement.
Q8: What are FHIR References and how are they used?
A: References link Resources together, enabling relationships between different healthcare entities (e.g., Patient to Encounter).
Q9: What is the difference between contained and referenced Resources?
A: Contained Resources are embedded within another Resource, while referenced Resources exist independently and are linked.
Q10: How does FHIR ensure data integrity and validation?
A: Through built-in validation rules, cardinality constraints, data type validation, and optional terminology binding.

Session 4: Foundational FHIR Part 2

Q1: What are the main FHIR Resources relevant to public health?
A: Patient, Observation, Condition, Immunization, Medication, Encounter, and Organization are key for public health use cases.
Q2: How does FHIR handle patient identification and demographics?
A: Through the Patient Resource which includes identifiers, demographics, contact information, and relationships.
Q3: What is the purpose of the Observation Resource in public health?
A: It represents clinical measurements, laboratory results, vital signs, and other health-related data points.
Q4: How does FHIR represent immunization data?
A: Through the Immunization Resource which includes vaccine details, administration dates, lot numbers, and status.
Q5: What is the significance of FHIR's Bundle Resource?
A: Bundles group multiple Resources together for batch operations, search results, and message exchanges.
Q6: How does FHIR handle organizational relationships?
A: Through the Organization Resource and its relationships with other Resources like HealthcareService and Practitioner.
Q7: What are FHIR's search capabilities and how do they work?
A: FHIR supports parameter-based searching, chaining, and filtering using standard HTTP query parameters.
Q8: How does FHIR handle versioning and history?
A: Through version IDs, meta.versionId, and the _history operation to track changes over time.
Q9: What is the purpose of FHIR's OperationOutcome Resource?
A: It provides detailed error information when FHIR operations fail, helping with debugging and error handling.
Q10: How does FHIR support different levels of data granularity?
A: Through optional elements, extensions, and the ability to include or exclude specific data based on use case requirements.

Session 5: Day 1 Wrap Up

Q1: What are the key takeaways from Day 1 regarding FHIR's role in public health?
A: FHIR provides a modern, standardized approach to healthcare data exchange that can significantly improve public health capabilities.
Q2: How do the foundational FHIR concepts apply to public health use cases?
A: Resources, Profiles, and Extensions enable flexible representation of public health data while maintaining standardization.
Q3: What are the main challenges in transitioning from legacy systems to FHIR?
A: Data mapping complexity, organizational change management, and ensuring data quality during migration.
Q4: How does FHIR support the different data needs of clinical vs. public health systems?
A: FHIR's flexibility allows customization for population-level analysis while maintaining individual patient data integrity.
Q5: What are the benefits of using FHIR for public health surveillance?
A: Real-time data access, standardized reporting, improved data quality, and better integration with clinical systems.
Q6: How does FHIR address the interoperability challenges faced by public health agencies?
A: Through standardized data formats, RESTful APIs, and built-in support for common healthcare terminologies.
Q7: What role do FHIR Profiles play in public health implementations?
A: Profiles ensure data consistency across jurisdictions and enable specific public health reporting requirements.
Q8: How does FHIR support the transition from batch to real-time data exchange?
A: Through its RESTful API design, event-driven architecture, and support for real-time notifications.
Q9: What are the key considerations for FHIR implementation planning in public health?
A: Stakeholder engagement, technical architecture, data governance, and phased implementation approach.
Q10: How does Day 1's content prepare participants for Day 2's advanced topics?
A: By establishing foundational knowledge of FHIR concepts, Resources, and basic operations needed for advanced implementations.

Session 6: FHIR Operations & Tech

Q1: What are FHIR Operations and how do they differ from standard REST operations?
A: FHIR Operations are custom actions that extend beyond CRUD operations, using the $operation syntax for complex workflows.
Q2: What is the purpose of the $validate operation in FHIR?
A: It validates Resources against Profiles, ensuring data conforms to specified constraints and business rules.
Q3: How does FHIR handle batch and transaction processing?
A: Through Bundle Resources with batch-type or transaction-type, enabling multiple operations in a single request.
Q4: What is the significance of FHIR's subscription mechanism?
A: It enables real-time notifications when Resources change, supporting event-driven architectures and public health surveillance.
Q5: How does FHIR support different authentication and authorization models?
A: Through OAuth 2.0, SMART on FHIR, and support for various security frameworks like TLS and JWT tokens.
Q6: What are FHIR's GraphQL capabilities and when would you use them?
A: FHIR GraphQL allows clients to specify exactly what data they need, reducing over-fetching and improving performance.
Q7: How does FHIR handle data persistence and storage?
A: FHIR doesn't specify storage, but implementations typically use relational databases, NoSQL, or hybrid approaches.
Q8: What is the purpose of FHIR's messaging framework?
A: It enables asynchronous communication between systems, supporting workflows that don't require immediate responses.
Q9: How does FHIR support different deployment models (cloud, on-premise, hybrid)?
A: FHIR's RESTful nature makes it cloud-friendly, while its flexibility allows deployment in various infrastructure models.
Q10: What are the key considerations for FHIR performance optimization?
A: Efficient indexing, query optimization, caching strategies, and appropriate use of pagination and search parameters.

Session 7: Implementation Guides

Q1: What is an Implementation Guide (IG) and what is its purpose?
A: An IG is a set of rules about how FHIR Resources are used to solve a particular problem, ensuring consistent implementation.
Q2: What are the main components of a FHIR Implementation Guide?
A: Profiles, Extensions, Value Sets, Code Systems, Examples, and narrative documentation.
Q3: How do Implementation Guides ensure interoperability across different systems?
A: By defining common Profiles, constraints, and terminologies that all implementing systems must follow.
Q4: What is the difference between a Profile and an Implementation Guide?
A: A Profile is a single constraint on a Resource, while an IG is a collection of Profiles and other artifacts for a complete solution.
Q5: How do Implementation Guides handle different use cases and requirements?
A: Through multiple Profiles, conditional requirements, and optional elements that can be implemented based on specific needs.
Q6: What is the role of Value Sets in Implementation Guides?
A: Value Sets define the specific codes and terminologies that must be used for particular data elements in the IG.
Q7: How do Implementation Guides support testing and validation?
A: Through conformance resources, test data, and validation tools that ensure implementations meet IG requirements.
Q8: What are some common public health Implementation Guides?
A: US Core, Vital Records, Public Health Case Reporting, and various disease-specific reporting IGs.
Q9: How do Implementation Guides evolve and maintain backward compatibility?
A: Through versioning strategies, deprecation policies, and migration guides that support gradual transitions.
Q10: What is the process for developing a new Implementation Guide?
A: Requirements gathering, use case analysis, Profile development, stakeholder review, and publication through HL7 processes.

Session 8: HL7 Processes

Q1: What is the HL7 standards development process and how does it work?
A: It's a consensus-based process involving workgroups, ballots, public comment periods, and iterative refinement of standards.
Q2: What are the different maturity levels in HL7 standards development?
A: Draft, Trial Use, Normative, and Informative, each representing increasing levels of stability and adoption.
Q3: How do HL7 Workgroups contribute to standards development?
A: Workgroups focus on specific domains, develop requirements, create specifications, and shepherd standards through the process.
Q4: What is the role of the HL7 Technical Steering Committee (TSC)?
A: The TSC provides technical oversight, approves workgroup charters, and ensures consistency across HL7 standards.
Q5: How does the HL7 ballot process work?
A: It involves formal review periods where stakeholders can comment on proposed standards, with responses and reconciliation.
Q6: What are HL7 Accelerators and how do they differ from Workgroups?
A: Accelerators focus on rapid implementation and adoption of standards, while Workgroups focus on standards development.
Q7: How does HL7 ensure international collaboration and harmonization?
A: Through affiliate organizations, joint projects, and participation in international standards development organizations.
Q8: What is the significance of HL7's open source approach to standards development?
A: It enables transparency, community participation, and faster iteration while maintaining quality and consensus.
Q9: How do HL7 processes support implementation and adoption?
A: Through implementation guides, reference implementations, testing tools, and educational resources.
Q10: What role does the HL7 Public Health Workgroup play in FHIR development?
A: It represents public health interests, develops public health-specific Profiles and IGs, and ensures FHIR meets public health needs.

Session 9: Next Steps

Q1: What are the key steps for planning a FHIR implementation in a public health organization?
A: Assess current systems, define requirements, select appropriate IGs, plan architecture, and establish governance.
Q2: How should organizations approach FHIR implementation - big bang or phased?
A: Phased approach is recommended, starting with pilot projects and gradually expanding to more complex use cases.
Q3: What are the essential skills and roles needed for FHIR implementation?
A: FHIR developers, data architects, clinical informaticists, project managers, and business analysts with healthcare domain knowledge.
Q4: How can organizations ensure data quality during FHIR implementation?
A: Through data mapping validation, testing with real data, and implementing quality checks and monitoring.
Q5: What are the key considerations for FHIR security and privacy?
A: Authentication, authorization, audit logging, data encryption, and compliance with regulations like HIPAA.
Q6: How should organizations approach testing and validation of FHIR implementations?
A: Use FHIR validation tools, create comprehensive test cases, and conduct end-to-end testing with real scenarios.
Q7: What are the ongoing maintenance requirements for FHIR implementations?
A: Regular updates to IGs, monitoring system performance, maintaining security patches, and staying current with FHIR versions.
Q8: How can organizations measure the success of their FHIR implementation?
A: Through metrics like data quality, system performance, user adoption, and achievement of business objectives.
Q9: What resources and communities are available for FHIR support?
A: HL7 documentation, FHIR chat, implementation guides, open source tools, and various user communities.
Q10: How can organizations stay current with FHIR developments and best practices?
A: Participate in HL7 workgroups, attend conferences, follow FHIR blogs, and engage with the FHIR community.
